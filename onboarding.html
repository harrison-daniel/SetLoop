SetLoop

Control any video with your voice. Loop sections, change speed, bookmark moments — all without touching your keyboard.

1
Grant microphone access
Click below to grant SetLoop mic access. The first time you use voice on a new site (YouTube, Vimeo, etc.), Chrome will ask one more time for that specific site — allow it and you're done.
2
Open any video and press Alt + V
Or click the SetLoop icon in your toolbar. A green dot confirms voice is live.
3
Say a command
Try "loop last 30 at 50 percent" to loop the last 30 seconds at half speed. Say "stop" when done.
Voice Commands
"loop last 30 at 50"Loop + slow down
"loop 1:30 to 2:00"Loop a range
"mark start" / "mark end"Set in/out points
"stop"End loop, restore speed
"wider" / "tighter"Loop start ±2s
"trim" / "extend"Loop end ±2s
"ramp" / "ramp off"Speed up each loop
"speed 75"Exact playback speed
"slower" / "faster"Adjust by 25%
"back 10"Rewind seconds
"bookmark"Save this moment
"copy link"Timestamped URL

How voice works. SetLoop uses Chrome's built-in Web Speech API for transcription. Chrome itself handles the audio pipeline — SetLoop never records, stores, or forwards audio. Voice requires an internet connection because Chrome sends audio to its speech service. Bookmarks stay on your device.

You're all set.
